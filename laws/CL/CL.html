‚òÖ Executive Summary

Companies Law 2018 ‚Äì Core Framework

Modern company law for DIFC: incorporation, governance, capital, directors' duties, and restructuring

Law No.5/2018

The DIFC Companies Law 2018 replaces the 2009 law and provides a complete legal framework for the incorporation, operation, governance, reorganisation and winding up of companies in the DIFC. It applies to all companies registered in the DIFC, including private companies, public companies, and special purpose vehicles. The Law is administered by the Registrar of Companies (part of DIFC Authority) and, for regulated financial services activities, concurrently by the DFSA under the Regulatory Law 2004.

üìú Provision

Art. 5 Regulatory Authority of the DFSA
üìú Provision
The DFSA has the function of keeping this Law under review and may make Rules under the Regulatory Law 2004 for any matter that by this Law is required or permitted to be prescribed by Rules.

Art. 6 Registrar of Companies
üìú Provision
The President of the DIFC shall appoint a Registrar of Companies and such deputy registrars as necessary. The Registrar shall keep a public register of companies and perform functions under this Law.
Art. 7 Powers of the Registrar
üìú Provision
The Registrar may issue guidance, make decisions on registration, strike off, restoration, and exercise powers conferred by this Law or any other DIFC law.
Art. 8 Public Register
üìú Provision
The Registrar shall maintain a public register of all companies, containing prescribed information, and allow inspection upon payment of any prescribed fee.
Art. 9 Language
üìú Provision
All documents filed with the Registrar must be in English or accompanied by an English translation certified as accurate. Arabic versions may also be filed.
Art. 10 Fees
üìú Provision
The Registrar may prescribe fees for filings, inspections, and other services. Fees are published on the DIFC website.

üìå Art.12 & 13
Private companies cannot offer shares to the public; public companies must have at least US$100,000 allotted share capital.
Art. 14 Method of forming company
üìú Provision
A company is formed by one or more persons subscribing to a memorandum and complying with registration requirements.
Art. 15 Memorandum of association
Required content
(a) Name, (b) type (public/private), (c) liability limited or unlimited, (d) guarantee amount (if guarantee company), (e) share capital and initial shareholding (if share company).
Effect
Memorandum binds the company and its members as if signed by each (contractual effect).
Art. 16 Articles of association
Provision
A company may adopt articles regulating its internal affairs. If no articles are registered, the model articles in Schedule 2 apply. Articles cannot contravene the memorandum.
üîó Model articles
Schedule 2 provides separate models for private and public companies. They apply automatically unless excluded or modified.
Art. 17 Company name
Rules
Name must end with appropriate suffix: ‚Äú(Private) Limited‚Äù / ‚Äú(Public) Limited‚Äù / ‚ÄúLimited by Guarantee‚Äù / ‚ÄúUnlimited‚Äù. Name must not be misleading or identical to existing name. Registrar may direct change within 6 months of registration if name too like another.
Art. 18 Registered office
Requirement
A company must have a registered office in the DIFC at all times. All communications and notices may be addressed to that address.
Art. 19 Registration application
Step 1
Prepare documents
Memorandum, articles (if any), statement of proposed director(s), registered office address, and any other required forms.
Step 2
Submit to Registrar
Deliver application and prescribed fee.
Step 3
Registrar checks
Registrar verifies compliance with requirements (name availability, proper documents).
Step 4
Certificate of incorporation
If satisfied, Registrar issues certificate with company number and date of incorporation. Company comes into existence on that date.
Art. 20 Effect of registration
Legal personality
From incorporation, the company is a body corporate with perpetual succession; capable of suing and being sued, holding property, and having an official seal.
Art. 21 Company‚Äôs registered number
Provision
Registrar assigns a unique number to each company. That number must appear on all business letters, order forms, and websites.
Art. 22 Amendment of constitution
Memorandum
May be amended by special resolution (75% majority) unless the memorandum provides otherwise.
Articles
May be amended by special resolution. Amendments bind all members.
Filing
Amendments must be filed with Registrar within 14 days; otherwise the company and officers commit an offence (Registrar may impose fines).
